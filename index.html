Conservation of Nature and Natural Resources (CONNAR)

is a Kenya-based consultancy established in 2011 to provide evidence-based solutions that support sustainable livelihoods and environmental stewardship.

We specialize in strengthening food systems, improving public health and nutrition, advancing climate resilience, and promoting responsible natural resource management. Our work is grounded in systems thinking and delivered through collaborative partnerships.

Our Services

CONNAR provides advisory, research, facilitation, and implementation support across interconnected development sectors.

Environmental & Social Impact Assessments (ESIA)

We conduct comprehensive assessments and audits to support regulatory compliance, reduce environmental risk, and enhance project sustainability.

Sustainable Food Systems Advisory

We support food system transformation initiatives through diagnostics, stakeholder engagement, and inclusive solution design.

Public Health & Nutrition Programming

We design and evaluate interventions that improve dietary outcomes, community health, and evidence-based policy formulation.

Climate Change Adaptation & Mitigation

We support climate risk analysis, adaptation planning, mitigation strategy development, and ecosystem restoration initiatives.

Research, Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning

We design practical MEL frameworks and tools that strengthen accountability, learning, and impact measurement.

Capacity Building & Training

We deliver workshops, training programs, and technical assistance that strengthen institutional and community capabilities.

Research & Consultancies

CONNAR has contributed to high-impact, multi-country research initiatives and consultancies focused on nutrition, food systems, fisheries, maternal and child health, and ecosystem sustainability.

High Impact Research & Evaluation 2023 – 2030

REACTS-IN Evaluation

Realizing Gender Equality, Attitudinal Change & Transformative Systems in Nutrition

McGill University (Lead Institution) | Global Affairs Canada

CONNAR is part of the international evaluation consortium assessing the REACTS-IN initiative across multiple countries. The project focuses on advancing gender-transformative approaches within nutrition systems. CONNAR contributes contextual analysis, evaluation design, and systems-based interpretation within the Kenyan component.

Food Systems Sustainable Fisheries 2020 – 2023

Samaki Salama Project

Securing Small-Scale Fisheries in Kenya for Healthy Nutrition and Ecosystems

Washington University in St. Louis | University of Rhode Island | Pwani University | Egerton University

CONNAR supported food systems diagnostics and fisheries-nutrition linkages, contributing to integrated strategies that strengthen nutrition security while promoting sustainable marine ecosystems.

Research Collaboration Fisheries & Nutrition 2019

SecureFish

Eco-sensitive Fish for Nutrition Security in Kenya

Washington University in St. Louis | University of Rhode Island | Pwani University | Egerton University

This study examined the contribution of fisheries to improved dietary outcomes while safeguarding aquatic ecosystems. CONNAR supported evidence generation and contextual systems analysis.

Multi-Country Program Public Health & Nutrition 2016 – 2021

ENRICH Project

Enhancing Nutrition Services to Improve Maternal and Child Health

University of Toronto | World Vision Canada

A five-country initiative across Africa and Asia. CONNAR supported nutrition systems strengthening and implementation research within Kenya, contributing to improved maternal and child health programming.

International Research Agriculture & Nutrition 2015 – 2018

HealthyLand Project

Crops for Healthy Diets: Linking Agriculture and Nutrition

LUANAR University | Universität Hohenheim | Giessen University | Egerton University | Makerere University

Funded by the German Federal Office for Agriculture and Food (BLE), this project examined agriculture-nutrition linkages across East and Southern Africa. CONNAR contributed to applied research and cross-country systems analysis.

Global Study Maternal & Child Health 2014 – 2016

International Human Milk Microbiome Project (IHMMP)

Washington State University | NIH

An international collaborative study focused on the standardization and cross-cultural analysis of human milk samples. CONNAR contributed to research coordination and local engagement within Kenya.
